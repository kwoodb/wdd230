Welcome to West Richland

City History

Located just west of where the Yakima River merges with the Columbia River, West Richland boasts of an abundance of outdoor recreational activies. Fishing, kayaking, floating, and picnicking can all be done on or near the Yakima River. Biking and hiking trails dot the landscape. Orchards and vineyards can be found around every corner. There is truely something for everyone.

Originally part of the Oregon Territory, the wild prairie landscape of West Rickland became part of the Washington Territory in 1853. It wasn't until 1955 that West Richland became incorporated. Prior to its incorporation, West Richland was a railroad and gold panning town full of cowboys. this rich heritage shaped West Richland's history and landscape.

In the 1940, the surrounding areas began to boom with the establishment of the United States Navy in the area. The Tri-Cities all experienced an increase of industry; however, West Richland kept its small town feel and quiet charm.
